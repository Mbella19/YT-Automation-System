## üéØ The Narration Formula

### For Each Scene:

```
1. WHO is involved? (Character names if known)
   "Sam...", "The detective...", "Marcus..."

2. WHAT is happening? (Plot action, not just visuals)
   "wakes up dizzy", "realizes the truth", "escapes"

3. WHY does it matter? (Story context within the scene)
   "It turns out...", "She's shocked to find...", "barely escaping..."

4. HOW does it unfold? (Progression within the timestamp)
   "approach ‚Üí retrieve ‚Üí take to hospital"
   "wakes ‚Üí sees floating pen ‚Üí gets distracted ‚Üí pen falls"

5. Make it ENGAGING
   "extremely dizzy", "shocked", "barely escaping", "breaks down"
```

## üîë Key Principles

### 1. Story Over Visuals
**Don't say:** "A person walks into a room"
**Do say:** "The detective enters the suspect's apartment searching for clues"

### 2. Character Names
**Don't say:** "A woman looks surprised"
**Do say:** "Sam looks surprised"

### 3. Plot Context (Within Timestamp!)
**Don't say:** "Someone is running"
**Do say:** "Marcus runs from the explosion"

### 4. Emotional Detail
**Don't say:** "A man talks"
**Do say:** "He breaks down confessing everything while tears stream down his face"

### 5. Cause and Effect (Within Scene)
**Don't say:** "A car crashes. A man is injured."
**Do say:** "The car slams into the barrier and John is thrown against the airbag"

## üéØ Scene Types & Narration Styles

### Action Scenes
**Focus on:**
- Dynamic verbs
- Stakes and danger
- Physical movement
- Tension

**Example:**
"Marcus sprints across the rooftop as bullets whiz past his head. He leaps over a gap between buildings barely making it to the other side as the edge crumbles beneath his feet."

### Dialogue/Drama Scenes
**Focus on:**
- Character emotions
- Revelations
- Relationship dynamics
- Reactions

**Example:**
"Sarah discovers her husband has been lying about his past. She confronts him with tears in her eyes and he finally admits the truth shattering everything she believed."

### Mystery/Suspense Scenes
**Focus on:**
- Discoveries
- Clues
- Character reactions
- Building tension

**Example:**
"The detective finds a hidden door behind the bookshelf. As she steps inside she discovers a wall covered in photos connecting all the victims revealing the killer's obsession."

### Emotional Scenes
**Focus on:**
- Character feelings
- Internal struggle
- Relationships
- Vulnerability

**Example:**
"John sits alone in the dark apartment holding his late wife's photo. Memories flood back and he finally allows himself to break down sobbing uncontrollably for the first time since her death."

## üéì Tips for Best Results

### 1. Video Selection
**Best videos for this system:**
- Clear storytelling
- Defined characters
- Linear or semi-linear plots
- Visible character actions

**Challenging videos:**
- Abstract/artistic films
- Heavy dialogue with minimal action
- Very fast-paced action (hard to capture in 8-10s)

### 2. Scene Length
- 8-10 seconds is optimal for story beats
- Too short (< 5s): Hard to establish context
- Too long (> 15s): Audio gets lengthy

### 3. Review Process
1. Check `scenes.json` after analysis
2. Read narrations - do they tell a story?
3. Are character names included?
4. Is it engaging to listen to?
